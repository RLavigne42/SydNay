## Chapter 1: First Revolution (Prime Gear Origins)
Elias Thorne arrives in Steamgard with a soot-blackened valise and a watchmaker’s patience, seeking out Silas Barnaby in a shuttered bell foundry where the furnaces sleep and anvils cool. Together, they listen for the hidden rhythm of the city and draft a master cog—the Prime Gear—meant to harmonize every clock, piston, and pressure valve into one precise cadence. Thorne etches the first teeth by hand while Barnaby tempers the alloy, both measuring the breath of steam to chart a force map that can lift the city into synchronized life. When the gear turns, it is a covenant as much as a mechanism, the first agreed heartbeat of Steamgard. The foundation keeps its chamber lit by amber lamps as apprentices trace its rotation, learning that innovation begins with reverence. Thorne’s slate still bears the smudged torque formula placeholder and the promise to measure and refine restraint, while the first quarter-turn arrives with a hush of steam—the city’s first sigh, the beginning of a revolution that does not roar. The documentarian notes that the Prime Gear is an oath: build with care, measure honestly, trust the steady turn.

## Chapter 2: Architectural Identity — The Face of Steamgard
Steamgard’s skyline reads as a living machine where functional elegance and Victorian grandeur are inseparable. The city’s identity is the beauty of function—ornate craftsmanship and exposed mechanics in shared equilibrium.

## Chapter 3: Society and the Culture of Innovation
Steamgard measures status by contribution to the Great Machine of progress. Social roles align by craft and discovery: inventors as cultural elite, artisans as skilled builders of mechanisms, adventurers as risk-takers feeding expeditions, and vendors as the backbone of street commerce. Fashion is functional, blending elegance with purpose—brass accents and mechanical elements signal profession. Merit is mechanical; social mobility comes from invention and craftsmanship rather than lineage. The city is a living workshop where identity is forged through contribution and style remains tethered to utility.
