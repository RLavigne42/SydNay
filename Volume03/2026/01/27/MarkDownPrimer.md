Greetings, fellow explorer! It is wise to check your map and tools before venturing further. Markdown is the essential syntax we use to structure our journals and map the terrain of our thoughts here in the digital forest. It is simple, yet powerful—much like the binary roots of these trees.

Here is a quick **Field Guide to Core Markdown Rules** to help you navigate your documentation:

### 1. Trail Markers (Headers)
Just as we mark the height of the canopy, use hashtags to denote the hierarchy of your content.
*   `# Header 1` (The highest peak)
*   `## Header 2` (A major landmark)
*   `### Header 3` (A specific trail marker)

### 2. Textures of the Flora (Emphasis)
To add nuance to your voice, like the difference between rough bark and soft moss:
*   **Bold**: Wrap text in double asterisks or underscores.
    *   Code: `**Bold Text**` or `__Bold Text__`
*   *Italics*: Wrap text in single asterisks or underscores.
    *   Code: `*Italic Text*` or `_Italic Text_`
*   ***Bold and Italic***: Triple them up.
    *   Code: `***Bold and Italic***`

### 3. Inventory & Waypoints (Lists)
Keep your gear organized or track your steps.
*   **Unordered List (Bullets)**: Use hyphens, asterisks, or plus signs.
    *   Code:
        ```markdown
        - Item A
        - Item B
          - Sub-item (indent with 2-4 spaces)
        ```
*   **Ordered List (Numbered)**: Use numbers followed by a period.
    *   Code:
        ```markdown
        1. First Step
        2. Second Step
        ```

### 4. The Underlying Code (Code Blocks)
Sometimes we must look at the raw data flowing through the Bitstream.
*   **Inline Code**: Wrap a snippet in single backticks.
    *   Code: `` `print("Hello World")` ``
*   **Code Block**: Use triple backticks to create a fenced block. You can specify the language for syntax highlighting.
    *   Code:
        ```markdown
        ```python
        def explore():
            return "Adventure awaits!"
        ```
        ```

### 5. Bridges & Portals (Links & Images)
Connect your current location to other parts of the network.
*   **Links**: `[Link Text](URL)`
*   **Images**: `![Alt Text](Image URL)` (Notice the exclamation mark at the start—it’s like spotting a rare bird!)

### 6. Echoes (Blockquotes)
To highlight a whisper from the wind or a quote from another explorer, use the greater-than sign.
*   Code: `> This is a blockquote.`
