# Worldbuilding Pillars Summary

Steamgard’s worldbuilding pillars distill how the Expo feels on the surface while hinting at the deeper mechanisms that keep the city’s wonders in motion, offering a quick reference for storytellers who want to balance spectacle with the hidden strata beneath it.

| Category | Surface Reading | Deep Reading |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Architecture | Brass spires, copper domes, and gear-lined streets define the skyline. | Load-bearing steam lattices and pressure corridors hide beneath every façade. |
| Innovation | Inventors unveil marvels in public demonstrations. | The Quarter-Turn’s timing protocols decide which breakthroughs may be shown. |
| Society | Crowds gather in festival unity around the Expo’s boulevards. | Guild oaths and maintenance crews quietly enforce order and survival. |
| Economy | Market stalls trade in gadgets, fuel, and artisanal craftwork. | Resource quotas and hidden tariffs keep the city’s pressure economy stable. |
| Mythos | Steamgard is celebrated as a city of endless ingenuity. | The city’s origin pact binds its future to precise rotations and rituals. |

## Legacy of the Quarter-Turn

The Quarter-Turn is remembered as the measured pause that keeps Steamgard from tearing itself apart. Every quarter-cycle, the city’s engines are realigned, exhibits are recalibrated, and the clockwork stewards sign the ledgers that permit the next wave of innovation.
