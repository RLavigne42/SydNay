## Philosophy of the Quarter-Turn
The quarter-turn is the smallest vow the city keeps with itself. A single rotation, neither stubborn nor surrendered, is the measure of a machine that listens. In Steamgard, the quarter-turn is a practice of restraint: the hinge that pauses, the valve that whispers, the pivot that offers just enough to keep the whole from splintering.

The artisans speak of it as a moral geometry. A full turn is certainty, a half turn is reversal, but a quarter-turn is choice. It is the moment in which the city allows friction to guide it toward grace.

> "A city of brass is only as strong as the iron that supports it, and iron needs to breathe." — *Field Note 882*
